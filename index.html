All Shinkansen Train Models!

The 500 series Shinkansen were originally used on the primary Nozomi service on Tōkaidō Shinkansen and Sanyo Shinkansen lines.

A total of E2 series trains were built between 1997 and 2010 before withdrawls of this Shinkansen type started in 2013. The 10-car trains can be coupled with E3 series trains.

E3 series Shinkansen was built to coincide with the opening of the new Akita Shinkansen “mini-shinkansen” line between Morioka and Akita and used on Komachi services.

E4 trains are bi-level Shinkansen with double-decker cars designed to accommodate high commuter traffic in the areas around Tokyo and other urban zones.

700 series were jointly designed by JR Central and JR-West. They were originally used on the Hakata Minami Line, the Sanyo Shinkansen, and the Tokaido Shinkansen.

The Shinkansen is perhaps the most visible sign of Japan’s technological advancement and — along with electronic consumer goods, cars, and Mt. Fuji — the most public symbol of Japan.
